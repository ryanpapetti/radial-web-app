You've never seen something like Radial before...

Radial makes clustering fun by leveraging your Spotify playlist data to group your tracks based on their audio similarity

1/8

Think of Radial as a musical exploration tool

Radial helps you find hidden patterns in the music you have across playlists you've made, patterns that are guaranteed to be unique to you

2/8

Radial works only with playlists you've created

The app crawls through your playlists, gathers your unique tracks, and makes queries to Spotify's API to retrieve relevant metadata for our app.

3/8

We ignore artist, album, language, and almost all demographic data

The only demographic data for tracks that Radial use are the durations of the tracks. Radial ignores these data to focus on the audio similarity of the tracks.

4/8

So how do we decide how two tracks are similar?

We take scaled track metadata from Spotify and run a standard distance formula, the sum of squared differences between the tracks' features.

5/8

Meet Your Clusterers

Once the data are structured accordingly, it is time to choose a clustering algorithm to group your data. We have two options for you

Agglomerative Hierarchical

Bottom-up tree-based clustering. Utilizes complete linkage for robustness. Find two tracks with smallest gap between the same feature, group and treat as one track - repeat until done.

KMeans

Place k points (centroids) randomly within data. Assign each track to closest centroid via standard distance formula. Update centroids with average of data from all tracks assigned. Repeat until centroids no longer change.

6/8

Now Your Clustering Types

Once the data are structured accordingly, it is time to choose a clustering algorithm to group your data. We have two options for you

Simple Clustering

Suitable for total track size (< 250). Produces five groups of tracks. Groups are less homogenous.

Standard Clustering

Suitable for all total track sizes. Produces nine groups of tracks.

Deep Clustering

Suitable for large track collections (> 1,500) tracks. Produces thirteen groups of tracks. Groups may be more niche or smaller.
